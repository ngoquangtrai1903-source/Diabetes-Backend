# DIABETES CLINICAL AND BEHAVIORAL RISK REGISTRY (2026)

## GROUP 1: CLINICAL INDICICES (DOCTOR MODE)

| Index | Classification / Thresholds | Risk Correlation | Biomedical Mechanism |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Age** | **Screening Start:** Age 35 for all asymptomatic adults. **High Risk:** Age $\geq$ 45. | Prevalence is lowest (1.9%) at age 20–24 and highest (24.8%) at age 75–79. For every year earlier T2D is diagnosed, dementia risk increases by 1.9%. | Aging is associated with a progressive decline in $\beta$-cell function and cumulative metabolic demand from long-term insulin resistance. |
| **Hypertension** | **Diagnosis:** $\geq$ 130/80 mmHg or on anti-hypertensive therapy. | Frequently clusters with obesity and dyslipidemia, acting as a major component of metabolic syndrome. | Hypertension and insulin resistance share common pathways; high blood pressure exacerbates microvascular damage and is often a result of visceral adiposity. |
| **Heart Disease** | **Screening:** History of CVD is an immediate indicator for screening regardless of age. | T2D increases the risk of Myocardial Infarction by 73%, Stroke by 54%, and Heart Failure by 84%. | Metabolic changes (hyperglycemia, dyslipidemia) and the build-up of ectopic fat in vital tissues like the heart lead to accelerated atherosclerosis and heart failure. |
| **Smoking History** | **Status:** Current smoker or history of habitual smoking. | Smoking is an established modifiable risk factor for T2D and GDM. Risk remains elevated in the years immediately following cessation. | Nicotine and oxidative stress disrupt insulin secretion and sensitivity; smoking promotes central adiposity, which is a key driver of insulin resistance. |
| **BMI (Asian Ancestry)** | **Overweight:** BMI $\geq$ 23 kg/m². **Obesity:** BMI $\geq$ 27.5 kg/m² (clinical context). | Every 1-kg weight loss confers a 16% reduction in T2D risk. Obesity alone causes a significant degree of insulin resistance. | Excess visceral fat triggers inflammation and the release of free fatty acids, leading to insulin resistance in skeletal muscle and liver (MASLD). |

## GROUP 2: COMMUNITY & LIFESTYLE INDICICES (HOME/COMMUNITY MODE)

### 1. High Blood Pressure & High Cholesterol (The Lethal Triad)
*   **Classification:** Hypertension ($\geq$ 130/80 mmHg) and Dyslipidemia (HDL < 35 mg/dL or Triglycerides > 250 mg/dL).
*   **Risk Correlation:** These factors cluster together to significantly heighten cardiovascular mortality, making the risk "disproportionately greater" at the higher end of the range.
*   **Mechanism:** Synergistic destruction of the endothelium. High cholesterol causes plaque buildup, while high glucose weakens vessel walls, leading to a 60% higher risk of any form of CVD in T2D patients.

### 2. History of Stroke or Heart Attack
*   **Classification:** Prior ischemic stroke, TIA, or myocardial infarction.
*   **Risk Correlation:** History of stroke/TIA with insulin resistance identifies individuals at extreme risk for both recurrent events and progression to overt diabetes.
*   **Mechanism:** Stroke and MI are markers of systemic macrovascular disease. Diabetes-related hyperosmolality and hypercoagulability increase the severity of these events.

### 3. Physical Activity (PhysActivity)
*   **Definition of Inactivity:** Performing physical activity less than 3 times a week.
*   **Risk Correlation:** Obtaining $\geq$ 150 min/week of moderate activity (e.g., brisk walking) reduces T2D incidence by 44% even if weight loss is not achieved.
*   **Mechanism:** Skeletal muscle is the primary site for insulin-mediated glucose disposal. Inactivity leads to muscular insulin resistance; regular activity increases GLUT4 translocation and improves glucose uptake.

### 4. Heavy Alcohol Consumption (HvyAlcoholConsump)
*   **Classification:** More than 2 drinks/day for men or 1 drink/day for women.
*   **Risk Correlation:** Excessive alcohol is a known trigger for hypoglycemia in those on medication and contributes to obesity.
*   **Mechanism:** Alcohol inhibits gluconeogenesis in the liver, interfering with the body’s ability to release glucose during fasting, and contributes to the accumulation of ectopic fat in the liver (MASLD).

### 5. Mental & General Health (Stress/Cortisol)
*   **Classification:** Self-reported poor mental health or high-stress environments.
*   **Risk Correlation:** Poor sleep quality (associated with mental distress) increases T2D risk by 40%.
*   **Mechanism:** Stress triggers the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, increasing **Cortisol**, which antagonizes insulin and directly raises blood glucose levels (gluconeogenesis).

### 6. Difficulty Walking (DiffWalk)
*   **Classification:** Difficulty or inability to walk or climb stairs due to physical or health issues.
*   **Risk Correlation:** A strong clinical indicator of advanced frailty, obesity-related mobility limitation, and high sedentary time.
*   **Mechanism:** Reduced mobility creates a vicious cycle of decreased energy expenditure, muscle atrophy, and worsening insulin resistance, which accelerates diabetes-related complications like neuropathy.

### 7. Fruit and Vegetable Consumption
*   **Classification:** Intake focusing on whole grains, legumes, fruits, and vegetables with minimal processed foods.
*   **Risk Correlation:** High-quality plant-based or Mediterranean diets are associated with significantly lower T1D and T2D progression risks.
*   **Mechanism:** Dietary fiber slows down the digestion of carbohydrates and the absorption of sugar, preventing postprandial glucose spikes and reducing the metabolic demand on $\beta$-cells.

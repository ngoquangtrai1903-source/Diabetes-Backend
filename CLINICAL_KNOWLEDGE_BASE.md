# CLINICAL MASTER: ADVANCED DIAGNOSIS, PHARMACOLOGY, AND COMPLICATIONS MANAGEMENT

## 1. DIAGNOSTIC INFRASTRUCTURE AND STAGING
The diagnosis of Diabetes Mellitus requires a validation of venous plasma glucose or A1C concentrations. In the absence of unequivocal hyperglycemia, diagnosis necessitates two abnormal results from either different tests or the same test at separate time points [1, 2].

### 1.1. Core Diagnostic Thresholds (Nonpregnant Adults)
| Clinical Status | A1C (NGSP Standard) | Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) | 2-h PG (75-g OGTT) | Random Plasma Glucose (RPG) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Normal** | < 5.7% (39 mmol/mol) | ≤ 99 mg/dL (5.5 mmol/L) | ≤ 139 mg/dL (7.7 mmol/L) | N/A |
| **Prediabetes** | 5.7 – 6.4% (39–47 mmol/mol) | 100 – 125 mg/dL (5.6 – 6.9 mmol/L) | 140 – 199 mg/dL (7.8 – 11.0 mmol/L) | N/A |
| **Diabetes** | **≥ 6.5% (48 mmol/mol)** | **≥ 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L)** | **≥ 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L)** | **≥ 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L)*** |

*Note: RPG diagnosis requires classic symptoms (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss) or hyperglycemic crisis (DKA/HHS) [3, 4].

### 1.2. Staging of Type 1 Diabetes (T1D)
T1D is categorized by the progression of autoimmunity and metabolic dysfunction [5, 6]:
*   **Stage 1:** Presence of ≥ 2 islet autoantibodies (GADA, IA-2A, ZnT8A); Normoglycemia; Presymptomatic. 5-year risk of symptomatic disease is ~44%.
*   **Stage 2:** Autoimmunity (usually multiple antibodies); Dysglycemia (FPG 100–125 mg/dL, 2-h PG 140–199 mg/dL, or A1C 5.7–6.4%); Presymptomatic. 5-year risk is ~75%.
*   **Stage 3:** Overt Hyperglycemia; Clinical symptoms (DKA, weight loss, polyuria).

### 1.3. Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM) Strategies (24–28 Weeks)
#### 1.3.1. One-Step Strategy (75-g OGTT)
Diagnosis is confirmed if **ANY** single value is met or exceeded [7]:
*   Fasting: 92 mg/dL (5.1 mmol/L)
*   1 hour: 180 mg/dL (10.0 mmol/L)
*   2 hours: 153 mg/dL (8.5 mmol/L)

#### 1.3.2. Two-Step Strategy
*   **Step 1:** 50-g GLT (nonfasting). If 1-h PG ≥ 130, 135, or 140 mg/dL (7.2, 7.5, or 7.8 mmol/L), proceed to Step 2.
*   **Step 2 (Carpenter-Coustan Criteria):** 100-g OGTT (fasting). Diagnosis requires **≥ 2 values** to be met or exceeded [7, 8]:
    *   Fasting: 95 mg/dL (5.3 mmol/L)
    *   1 hour: 180 mg/dL (10.0 mmol/L)
    *   2 hours: 155 mg/dL (8.6 mmol/L)
    *   3 hours: 140 mg/dL (7.8 mmol/L)

## 2. PHARMACOLOGICAL PROTOCOLS AND RATIONALE

### 2.1. Metformin: The Biguanide Standard
*   **Indication:** First-line for T2D and high-risk prediabetes (BMI ≥35, Age 25–59, A1C ≥6.0%) [9].
*   **Renal Dosing (eGFR Thresholds):**
    *   **eGFR 45–60:** Continue; monitor renal function annually.
    *   **eGFR 30–45:** Do not initiate; if current user, evaluate 50% dose reduction.
    *   **eGFR < 30:** Strictly contraindicated.
*   **Safety Monitoring:** Assess Vitamin B12 levels annually if on Metformin for > 4 years or if patient exhibits anemia or peripheral neuropathy [10].

### 2.2. Targeted Comorbidity Selection (SGLT2i and GLP-1 RA)
#### 2.2.1. Heart Failure (HFrEF/HFpEF)
*   **Priority:** SGLT2 Inhibitors (SGLT2i).
*   **Rationale:** SGLT2i reduce heart failure hospitalization and provide cardiorenal protection independently of glycemic control [11, 12].

#### 2.2.2. Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
*   **Priority:** SGLT2i or GLP-1 RA.
*   **Clinical Grading of CKD:**
    *   **Normal:** UACR < 30 mg/g.
    *   **Microalbuminuria (A2):** UACR 30 – 299 mg/g.
    *   **Macroalbuminuria (A3):** UACR ≥ 300 mg/g.
*   **Intervention:** For patients with hypertension and UACR ≥ 300 mg/g, prioritize RAS blockade (ACEi or ARB) [Previous Clinical context].

#### 2.2.3. ASCVD and Obesity
*   **Priority:** GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (GLP-1 RA) with proven CV benefit.
*   **Weight Management:** GLP-1 RAs provide "Significant Loss" of weight, which is the key mediator of diabetes delay [13].

### 2.3. Basal Insulin Initiation and Titration
*   **Initiation Dose:** 10 Units/day OR 0.1 – 0.2 Units/kg/day [Previous Clinical context].
*   **Weekly Titration:**
    *   Increase by **2 Units every 3 days** based on FPG.
    *   Target FPG: 80 – 130 mg/dL.
    *   **Hypoglycemia Rule:** If FPG < 70 mg/dL, reduce dose by 10%–20% or 4 units [Previous Clinical context].

## 3. CHRONIC COMPLICATION GRADING AND EXAM STEPS

### 3.1. Diabetic Retinopathy (DR)
*   **Clinical Grading:**
    *   **Any DR:** Sign of microvascular damage (~23% prevalence) [14, 15].
    *   **Proliferative DR (PDR):** Neovascularization present; requires urgent referral (~6% prevalence) [15].
    *   **Macular Oedema (DME):** Retinal thickening involving the fovea (~5% prevalence) [15].
*   **Screening:** T1D starts within 5 years of onset; T2D starts at diagnosis [16].

### 3.2. Diabetic Foot and Neuropathy
*   **Physical Exam Steps:**
    1.  **Visual Inspection:** Check for fungal infections, dry skin, callus, or deformities [Previous Clinical context].
    2.  **10-g Monofilament Test:** Apply perpendicular pressure at the plantar surface of the 1st, 3rd, and 5th metatarsal heads until the filament buckles.
    3.  **Result Interpretation:** Failure to feel at ≥ 1 site = **Loss of Protective Sensation (LOPS)**.
*   **Risk Categorization:**
    *   **Low:** Intact sensation/pulses.
    *   **Moderate:** LOPS or deformity.
    *   **High:** History of ulcer/amputation or Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD) [Previous Clinical context].

## 4. EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

### 4.1. Severe Hypoglycemia (Level 3)
*   **Definition:** Altered mental status requiring assistance [Previous Emergency context].
*   **Clinical Management:**
    1.  **Glucagon:** Injectable or nasal spray if patient is unconscious or cannot swallow.
    2.  **Rationale:** Glucagon stimulates hepatic glycogenolysis to restore blood glucose levels [17].

### 4.2. Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) Management
*   **Trigger Threshold:** Blood Glucose ≥ 240 mg/dL with illness symptoms [18].
*   **Diagnostic Criteria:** Hyperglycemia, Ketosis (blood/urine), and Metabolic Acidosis.
*   **Clinical Action:** Hospitalization is mandatory. Treatment includes aggressive fluid resuscitation (normal saline), potassium replacement (unless K+ > 5.2), and continuous IV regular insulin infusion (typically 0.1 U/kg/hr) [Previous Emergency context].

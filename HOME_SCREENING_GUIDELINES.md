# HOME ACTION GUIDE: DIABETES SCREENING AND LIFESTYLE MANAGEMENT

## 1. RECOGNIZING YOUR BODY'S SIGNALS
Diabetes often develops silently. You must watch for these "Subjective Symptoms" that indicate your blood sugar is too high:

### 1.1. Common Warning Signs
*   **Extreme Thirst (Polydipsia):** Feeling like you cannot drink enough water [19].
*   **Frequent Bathroom Trips (Polyuria):** Especially needing to pee several times during the night [19].
*   **Sudden Weight Loss:** Losing weight without trying, as your body burns fat for energy it cannot get from food [19, 20].
*   **Vision Changes:** Everything looks blurry or out of focus [19].
*   **Slow Healing:** A cut or bruise that stays on your skin for weeks without getting better [21].
*   **Acanthosis nigricans:** Dark, velvety skin patches appearing around your neck or armpits—this is a sign of "Insulin Resistance" [21].

## 2. CALCULATING YOUR RISK
You should get a professional blood test at your doctor's office if you are **age 35 or older** [22]. If you are younger, get tested if your BMI is 25 or higher (23 for Asian Americans) AND you have one of these risk factors [22, 23]:
*   A parent or brother/sister has diabetes.
*   You are physically active less than 3 times a week.
*   You have ever had "Gestational Diabetes" during pregnancy.
*   You have been told you have a "Fatty Liver" (NAFLD/MASLD).

## 3. THE "GOLDEN NUMBERS" FOR LIFESTYLE CHANGE
If you have prediabetes, you can delay or even reverse it by hitting these targets:

### 3.1. Physical Activity Plan
*   **150 Minutes:** Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate activity (like brisk walking) every week [24, 25].
*   **3 Times a Week:** Spread your exercise out. Do not let more than 2 days go by without moving [24].
*   **Break the Sit:** Stand up and move every 30 minutes to help your body process sugar better [25].

### 3.2. Weight and Nutrition
*   **5% to 7% Loss:** This is the target weight loss to stop diabetes. If you weigh 200 lbs, that is only 10–14 lbs [24, 26].
*   **Whole Foods:** Focus on whole grains, beans, nuts, fruits, and vegetables. Avoid processed foods and sugary drinks [27].

### 3.3. Sleep Hygiene
*   **7 Hours:** This is the ideal sleep time. Sleeping less than 6 hours or more than 9 hours increases your risk for type 2 diabetes by 50% [28].
*   **Quality Matters:** Poor sleep increases your risk of prediabetes turning into full diabetes by 40% [28].

## 4. MANAGING BLOOD SUGAR AT HOME
### 4.1. Standard Daily Targets
*   **Before a meal:** 80 to 130 mg/dL [29].
*   **2 hours after starting a meal:** Less than 180 mg/dL [29].

### 4.2. Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM) Goals
If you use a wearable sensor, aim for these targets:
*   **Time-in-Range (70–180 mg/dL):** More than 70% of the day.
*   **Time-Below-Range (< 70 mg/dL):** Less than 4% of the day (about 1 hour) [Previous Emergency context].

## 5. EMERGENCY ACTION PROTOCOLS

### 5.1. Low Blood Sugar: The 15/15 Rule
If you feel shaky, sweaty, or dizzy and your meter shows **less than 70 mg/dL**, follow these steps:
1.  **Eat 15 grams of fast carbs:** 4 oz of juice, 1 tablespoon of honey, or glucose tabs [30].
2.  **Wait 15 minutes.**
3.  **Check your sugar again.**
4.  **Repeat** if it is still below 70 mg/dL [Previous Emergency context].

### 5.2. High Blood Sugar: The Red Flag
*   **Danger Level:** If your sugar is **240 mg/dL or higher**, especially when you are sick, you must check your urine for "Ketones" using a kit from the pharmacy [18].
*   **DKA Emergency:** If you have high ketones along with stomach pain, vomiting, or fruity-smelling breath, **go to the Emergency Room immediately**. This is Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) [18].
